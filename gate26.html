GATE 2026

Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) is a prestigious national-level examination. The examination assesses candidates for a comprehensive understanding of different undergraduate-level subjects in Engineering, Technology, Science, Commerce, Arts, Architecture, and Humanities.

GATE 2026 is being conducted by IISc and all IITs on behalf of the National Coordination Board (NCB), Department of Higher Education, Ministry of Education (MoE), Government of India. IIT Guwahati is the Organizing Institute for GATE 2026. click to open ⬇️

Opportunities

  • Candidates who qualify can seek admission with possible financial assistance to Master's, Direct Doctoral, and Doctoral programs in relevant branches.
  • GATE score is also used by colleges for postgraduate admission without MoE scholarship.
  • Several Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs) use GATE score for recruitment.

Eligibility Criteria

Before applying, candidates must ensure they meet the eligibility criteria for GATE 2026. Students currently in 3rd year or higher or already completed degrees in relevant fields are eligible.

  • Engineering/Technology/Architecture/Science/Commerce/Arts/Humanities
  • Professional Society Examinations equivalent to B.E./B.Tech./B.Arch.
  • Foreign degree holders: Must be in 3rd year or higher or completed Bachelor’s program.

Required Documents

  • Good quality photograph and signature
  • Valid Photo ID (original to be produced)
  • Category/PwD/Dyslexic certificates, if applicable
  • Relevant Annexures for scribe/compensatory time, if applicable

NOTE

The test papers will be in English. Candidates opting to appear in TWO test papers must have a primary choice of test paper. The second test paper must be chosen from the allowed combinations given in the table above. Combinations other than the listed ones are NOT allowed in the initial (Regular and Extended) windows of registration. We may open up additional two-paper combinations at a later date, which will be notified on the GATE 2026 website. This is subject to the availability of infrastructure and scheduling feasibility.

The examination centre of a candidate for the second test paper may be different (but in the same city) from that for the first paper due to infrastructure and scheduling constraints. GATE 2026 shall NOT be liable for any legal obligations arising out of this issue.

Under unforeseen circumstances, certain combinations may be removed from the list in the above table at a later date. In such cases, the fee paid towards the second test paper will be refunded to the candidates.
